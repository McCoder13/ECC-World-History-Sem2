Age of Exploration

Not everything was positive during the Age of Exploration, however. Exploitation and enslavement of native people were not uncommon during the time. Diseases, such as smallpox, were also spread by the Europeans, exposing the indigenous populations who had never come in contact with them.

Although the Age of Exploration brought about significant advancements in navigation, exploration, and international trade, resulting in the discovery of new lands, colonization of the Americas, and numerous scientific and technological breakthroughs. This period was not without its negative consequences, as exploitation, enslavement, and the spread of diseases had devastating effects on native populations. Overall, the Age of Exploration marked a crucial turning point in world history, leading to the discovery of America and laying the foundations for the world stage we know today.
