Age of Revolutions

During the Age of Revolutions, which spanned the late 18th and early 19th centuries, significant political and social changes were occurring in Europe and the Americas. These transformations were led by a series of political and social revolutions that aimed to overthrow the existing power structures and establish more democratic systems of government.

Among the most well-known revolutions of this period were the American Revolution, the French Revolution, and the English Revolution. The American Revolution, which began in 1775 and lasted until 1783, was the first of these revolutions, and it was closely followed by the French Revolution, which began in 1789 and aimed to fundamentally transform French society by overthrowing the monarchy. The English Revolution, which took place in the mid-17th century, was another significant event that saw the overthrow of the monarchy and the establishment of a republic.
