World War Two

Fought on multiple fronts across Europe, Africa, Asia, and the Pacific, the war involved major military powers, including the United States, the Soviet Union, Great Britain, and Germany. The United States' entry into the war after the Japanese attack on Pearl Harbor in 1941 marked a significant shift in the balance of power.

The war ultimately ended with the unconditional surrender of Germany and Japan and the establishment of the United Nations, intending to promote global peace and security. As one of the deadliest conflicts in human history, it resulted in the loss of millions of lives, both military and civilian, the destruction of entire cities, and geopolitical changes, including the emergence of the United States and the Soviet Union as superpowers and the beginning of the Cold War.
