Age of Thought Revolutions

During the late 19th and 20th centuries, figures such as Karls Marx, Charles Darwin, and Sigmund Freud challenged the traditional way of thought with terrifying effectiveness in today's modern society.

Karls Marx, born in 1818 in Germany, completely rejected any belief in God. He believed that man was supreme with no other being higher than it. He wrote about communism and how capitalism was a failing idea and communism was the answer. He also believed that violence was a necessary tool to achieve his goals which he viewed as the only possible correct way of thinking.
